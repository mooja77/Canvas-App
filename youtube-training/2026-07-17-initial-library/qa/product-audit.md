# QualCanvas product and audience audit

## Product position

QualCanvas is a visual qualitative-data-analysis workspace. It keeps transcripts, open-text data, codes, coded excerpts, cases, memos, analytical views and exports connected on a spatial canvas. Its strongest differentiator is traceability: a researcher can move from an analytical claim back through a memo and code to the source passage without leaving the workspace.

## Primary users and sectors

- Graduate, doctoral and postdoctoral researchers conducting interview, field-note or open-text analysis.
- Principal investigators, methods lecturers and research labs coordinating shared codebooks and review.
- UX and service researchers synthesising interviews across products, journeys or participant groups.
- Public-health, social-work, education, policy and nonprofit teams handling sensitive qualitative material.
- Research administrators and institutional buyers concerned with governance, processors, access and AI boundaries.

## Core first-time journey

Create a canvas, choose a blank or methodology-informed starting point, add an approved transcript, create or refine a code, apply it to a contextual passage, write a memo and inspect the developing codebook. The current interface supports this journey but presents many advanced controls early; the product tour, Help menu, guide and command palette are important orientation surfaces.

## Everyday tasks

- Add, rename, review and navigate transcripts or other supported sources.
- Create, define, organise and apply codes.
- Review coded excerpts and refine the codebook.
- Arrange nodes, follow connections, search and use the navigator.
- Write analytical memos and capture methodological decisions.
- Export readable reports, workbooks, images and exchange files.

## Advanced workflows

- Text search, word clouds and sentiment exploration.
- Statistics, co-occurrence, Boolean coding queries and clustering.
- Framework matrices, comparisons and theme maps.
- Cases, cross-case analysis, hierarchies and code weighting.
- Intercoder agreement, methods statements and AI-agreement review.
- Real-time presence, sharing, presentation mode and repository-style cross-canvas work.
- AI-assisted code suggestions, summarisation and framework synthesis when the study permits provider processing.

## Likely points of confusion

- The Tools, Analyze and AI menus expose many choices before a new researcher knows which question each tool answers.
- “Code” may be confused with software code; training should consistently call it a qualitative code and show a transcript passage.
- Counts, sentiment and agreement scores can be mistaken for conclusions or validity measures.
- Share links/codes can be treated casually even though they grant access.
- AI availability can be mistaken for ethics approval. The videos must state that consent, lawful basis, processor terms and institutional policy govern use.
- Exported files leave application access controls and inherit the sensitivity of the source data.
- The repository contains a Training Center component and API, but it is not currently wired into the live canvas UI; videos must not claim that surface is available.

## Existing help and marketing content

- A comprehensive public `/guide` with current screenshots and workflow explanations.
- A 22-step full product tour available on demand, plus `?demo=true` auto-advance support.
- A two-screen onboarding flow, checklist, command palette and keyboard-shortcut modal.
- Methodology field-guide chapters, trust/AI disclosures, privacy and security pages.
- Existing raw recordings and earlier marketing screencasts that are useful as historical references but are not the source masters for this library.

## Privacy, safety and professional boundaries

- Use only synthetic demo material in capture and never show saved browser identity, autofill, customer data, tokens, keys or private email.
- QualCanvas supports research work; it does not grant permission to upload participant data. Consent, ethics approval, data-processing terms and institutional policy remain controlling.
- AI is opt-in per action. Relevant content is sent to a selected supported provider for the request; researchers must minimise, review and disclose use.
- QualCanvas records AI usage metadata rather than prompt/response content according to the current public policy, but provider and protocol requirements still apply.
- Agreement metrics support calibration and discussion; they do not validate an interpretation.
- Exported files must be stored, shared and deleted under the same protocol as the source material.
