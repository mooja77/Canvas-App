# QualCanvas for UX, Service and Applied Research

**Outcome:** Combine interviews, open-text responses, visible analysis and research memos in an applied-research workflow.

**Audience:** Not made for kids  
**Language:** English (Ireland)  
**Voice:** en-IE-EmilyNeural

## Scene plan

### 1. Move from evidence to a responsible decision (0:00)

- Visual: Original QualCanvas brand frame
- On-screen callout: Interviews and open text become a visible reasoning trail
- Narration: UX, service and applied researchers often need to move quickly without losing the chain of evidence. QualCanvas brings source material, coding, patterns and research memos into one visual workspace for review and handoff.

### 2. Start with the job your research must support (0:16)

- Visual: captures/10-customers.png
- On-screen callout: Product, service, policy and programme questions need different outputs
- Narration: Define the decision, audience and method before opening the tool. A discovery study, service evaluation and programme review may use similar interviews, but they do not automatically support the same claim.

### 3. Bring in interviews and field material (0:30)

- Visual: captures/14-transcript-menu.png
- On-screen callout: Paste, type or upload supported transcript files
- Narration: Add interview transcripts, workshop notes or other approved text sources. Use fictional or de-identified labels, and keep consent and data-handling rules aligned with the environment where the research was collected.

### 4. Add open-text survey responses (0:46)

- Visual: captures/31-survey-import.png
- On-screen callout: Use CSV import for consistent batches of responses
- Narration: For open-text survey material, import a clean CSV and map the meaningful fields. Do not bring in identifying columns merely because the survey export included them.

### 5. Keep the evidence connected to interpretation (0:59)

- Visual: captures/13-canvas-workspace.png
- On-screen callout: Source → excerpt → code → pattern → memo
- Narration: Code excerpts, group related ideas and preserve links back to the source. The visual structure helps a stakeholder see how a recommendation developed, while the researcher remains responsible for context and rival explanations.

### 6. Choose a handoff for the audience (1:14)

- Visual: captures/20-export-menu.png
- On-screen callout: Report, workbook, image or exchange package
- Narration: Use a report for narrative review, a workbook for structured checking, an image for explaining relationships or QDPX for compatible project exchange. Remove participant material that the recipient is not authorised to receive.

### 7. Fast research can still be traceable (1:30)

- Visual: Original QualCanvas brand frame
- On-screen callout: Frame · collect · code · challenge · memo · hand off
- Narration: Frame the decision, collect only what you can govern, code visibly, challenge the emerging account, memo the reasoning and hand off the right level of evidence. Follow the full QualCanvas training path for step-by-step practice.
