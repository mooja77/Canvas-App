# QualCanvas in 2 Minutes | From Transcript to Defensible Theme

**Outcome:** Understand how QualCanvas connects transcripts, codes, themes, memos and exports in one visual workflow.

**Audience:** Not made for kids  
**Language:** English (Ireland)  
**Voice:** en-IE-EmilyNeural

## Scene plan

### 1. From transcript to defensible theme (0:00)

- Visual: Original QualCanvas brand frame
- On-screen callout: See the interpretation—not just a list of codes
- Narration: QualCanvas is a visual workspace for qualitative researchers. It keeps transcripts, codes, analytical connections and research memos together, so you can see how an interpretation developed instead of reconstructing it at the end.

### 2. Built around qualitative work (0:16)

- Visual: captures/01-landing-hero.png
- On-screen callout: Code interviews, find themes and write memos you can defend
- Narration: The product is designed for interview studies, field notes, open-text survey responses and other qualitative material. The first goal is simple: bring a source into a workspace without turning your analysis into a spreadsheet.

### 3. Keep the evidence in view (0:32)

- Visual: captures/13-canvas-workspace.png
- On-screen callout: Transcript excerpts, codes and links share one canvas
- Narration: On the canvas, each source remains readable. Codes sit beside the passages they describe, and the links between them make recurring patterns visible across the study.

### 4. Move from coding to analysis (0:43)

- Visual: captures/18-analyze-menu.png
- On-screen callout: Search, frequency, co-occurrence, queries, clustering and frameworks
- Narration: When you are ready to analyse, the tools are grouped by purpose. Explore text, inspect coding patterns, compare cases or build a framework matrix without losing the path back to the source.

### 5. Keep the codebook reviewable (0:58)

- Visual: captures/23-codebook.png
- On-screen callout: Definitions, colours, hierarchy and evidence stay together
- Narration: The codebook gives the project a shared vocabulary. Review definitions, counts and hierarchy, then refine the structure as your interpretation changes.

### 6. Take the work with you (1:09)

- Visual: captures/20-export-menu.png
- On-screen callout: Word, web, text, Excel, image and QDPX exports
- Narration: Export a report for writing, an Excel workbook for review, an image for a presentation, or QDPX for exchange with compatible qualitative-analysis tools. Your work is not trapped in the canvas.

### 7. Start with one real transcript (1:24)

- Visual: Original QualCanvas brand frame
- On-screen callout: Explore the guide or create a free project at qualcanvas.com
- Narration: Start with one transcript and one research question. Build the interpretation in small, reviewable steps, and use the guide whenever you need the next task. Visit qualcanvas dot com to begin.
