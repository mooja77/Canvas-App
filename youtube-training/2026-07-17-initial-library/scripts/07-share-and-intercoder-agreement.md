# Share a QualCanvas Project and Check Intercoder Agreement

**Outcome:** Share work intentionally, understand roles and use agreement metrics as a discussion aid.

**Audience:** Not made for kids  
**Language:** English (Ireland)  
**Voice:** en-IE-EmilyNeural

## Scene plan

### 1. Collaborate without losing control (0:00)

- Visual: Original QualCanvas brand frame
- On-screen callout: Share the right workspace with the right person for the right task
- Narration: Collaboration works best when access has a purpose. Decide whether someone needs to view, code, review or manage the project before sharing a canvas or inviting them into a team workflow.

### 2. Plan the handoff before the invitation (0:13)

- Visual: captures/05-for-teams.png
- On-screen callout: Define roles, codebook ownership and review points
- Narration: For a team study, agree who owns the codebook, when coders work independently and how disagreements will be resolved. Software can show activity; it cannot make those methodological decisions for you.

### 3. Share from the canvas (0:28)

- Visual: captures/21-share-canvas.png
- On-screen callout: Use the available share controls and treat codes as credentials
- Narration: Open Share from the workspace and use the available access controls. A share code or link grants access, so send it through an appropriate channel and revoke or rotate it when the collaboration ends.

### 4. Calibrate with a common codebook (0:41)

- Visual: captures/23-codebook.png
- On-screen callout: Definitions and examples should be clear before independent coding
- Narration: Before checking agreement, confirm that every coder worked from the same definitions and unit of analysis. A metric cannot rescue an ambiguous codebook or inconsistent passage boundaries.

### 5. Use agreement as a prompt for discussion (0:55)

- Visual: captures/25-intercoder.png
- On-screen callout: Kappa or alpha summarises consistency; it does not validate the interpretation
- Narration: The intercoder view can calculate agreement from comparable coding work. Treat the score as a signal: inspect the disagreements, discuss the reasoning and revise the codebook when the pattern reveals genuine ambiguity.

### 6. Keep the discussion connected to excerpts (1:09)

- Visual: captures/13-canvas-workspace.png
- On-screen callout: Resolve differences by returning to the source
- Narration: When coders disagree, return to the passage and its context. Record the resolution in a memo so the next similar case is handled consistently and the methods account remains transparent.

### 7. Agreement supports judgement (1:23)

- Visual: Original QualCanvas brand frame
- On-screen callout: Roles · shared definitions · independent work · discussion
- Narration: A sound collaboration has clear roles, shared definitions, a period of independent work and a documented discussion of differences. QualCanvas supports that process; the research team remains responsible for it.
