# QualCanvas for Methods Teaching and Research Teams

**Outcome:** Use shared definitions, visible evidence and structured review to teach coding or coordinate a research team.

**Audience:** Not made for kids  
**Language:** English (Ireland)  
**Voice:** en-IE-EmilyNeural

## Scene plan

### 1. Teach the reasoning—not just the clicks (0:00)

- Visual: Original QualCanvas brand frame
- On-screen callout: Visible evidence makes coding decisions discussable
- Narration: In a methods course or research team, the valuable conversation is why a passage was coded in a particular way. QualCanvas keeps the excerpt, definition and developing structure visible enough to discuss together.

### 2. Use an institutional path for governed work (0:14)

- Visual: captures/06-for-institutions.png
- On-screen callout: Teaching, labs and research groups need appropriate access and support
- Narration: The institutions page outlines options for methods teaching, labs and governed deployments. Confirm procurement, hosting and data-processing requirements before a cohort uploads any real participant material.

### 3. Give learners a clear shared codebook (0:29)

- Visual: captures/23-codebook.png
- On-screen callout: Definition, inclusion rule, exclusion rule and examples
- Narration: Begin a calibration exercise with explicit definitions and passage boundaries. Ask learners to code independently before showing the class distribution, so the result reflects their decisions rather than group influence.

### 4. Compare and discuss disagreement (0:44)

- Visual: captures/25-intercoder.png
- On-screen callout: Use a score to locate teaching moments
- Narration: Agreement metrics can identify codes or excerpts that need discussion. Open the disagreements, ask each coder to explain the reasoning and revise the codebook only when the discussion supports a clearer rule.

### 5. Control access to teaching material (0:58)

- Visual: captures/21-share-canvas.png
- On-screen callout: Use synthetic or licensed examples for classroom practice
- Narration: For teaching, prefer synthetic transcripts or material licensed for classroom use. Share only with the intended cohort, and remove access when the exercise ends.

### 6. Turn calibration into a team workflow (1:10)

- Visual: captures/05-for-teams.png
- On-screen callout: Roles, review points and handoffs remain explicit
- Narration: The same structure supports a research team: define roles, schedule independent coding, review disagreements and document decisions. Real-time tools make coordination easier, but the team still owns the method.

### 7. Make interpretation discussable (1:26)

- Visual: Original QualCanvas brand frame
- On-screen callout: Shared evidence · independent judgement · documented review
- Narration: Whether you are teaching a class or coordinating a lab, keep the evidence shared, the initial judgement independent and the review documented. That builds confidence without pretending qualitative interpretation is mechanical.
