# Write Research Memos and Keep an Audit Trail in QualCanvas

**Outcome:** Write useful analytical memos and document decisions, ethics boundaries and changes over time.

**Audience:** Not made for kids  
**Language:** English (Ireland)  
**Voice:** en-IE-EmilyNeural

## Scene plan

### 1. Make the reasoning visible (0:00)

- Visual: Original QualCanvas brand frame
- On-screen callout: A memo records what changed in your interpretation—and why
- Narration: A code tells you how a passage was classified. A research memo records the analytical reasoning around that decision: what you noticed, what changed and what evidence might challenge the interpretation.

### 2. Create a memo beside the evidence (0:14)

- Visual: captures/16-memo-menu.png
- On-screen callout: Write the claim, the uncertainty and the next check
- Narration: Open Memo and place it near the relevant source or code. A useful memo names the emerging idea, records uncertainty and ends with a next action such as comparing another case or revisiting a definition.

### 3. Use position as part of the explanation (0:28)

- Visual: captures/13-canvas-workspace.png
- On-screen callout: Group related sources, codes, memos and outputs without hiding their links
- Narration: The canvas lets you arrange reasoning spatially. Keep a memo close to the evidence it interprets, and use groups carefully so organisation supports the argument rather than replacing it.

### 4. Keep ethics decisions in the workflow (0:42)

- Visual: captures/24-ethics.png
- On-screen callout: Consent, retention and AI use are analytical boundaries
- Narration: The Ethics view helps keep governance questions visible. Record the boundaries that matter to the study, but remember that software prompts do not replace consent terms, ethics approval or institutional policy.

### 5. Use methods and quality tools deliberately (0:56)

- Visual: captures/17-tools-menu.png
- On-screen callout: Methods statement, excerpts, codebook and agreement checks
- Narration: The Tools menu gathers supporting views for methods and quality work. Use excerpts and the codebook to inspect evidence, and generate a methods statement only after confirming that it accurately describes what you did.

### 6. Export an account of the work (1:11)

- Visual: captures/20-export-menu.png
- On-screen callout: Reports and disclosure files support review and handoff
- Narration: Exported reports can support supervision, team review or writing. When AI was used, include the relevant disclosure and retain the human decisions behind accepted or rejected suggestions.

### 7. A defensible study leaves a trail (1:25)

- Visual: Original QualCanvas brand frame
- On-screen callout: Decision · reason · evidence · revision
- Narration: You do not need to document every click. Document the decisions that changed the analysis: the reason, the evidence and any later revision. That is the trail another researcher can follow.
