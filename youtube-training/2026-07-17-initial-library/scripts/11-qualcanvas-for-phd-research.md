# QualCanvas for PhD and Dissertation Research | A Defensible Analysis Trail

**Outcome:** See how a doctoral researcher can organise a method, analysis trail, supervision handoff and final outputs.

**Audience:** Not made for kids  
**Language:** English (Ireland)  
**Voice:** en-IE-EmilyNeural

## Scene plan

### 1. A dissertation needs an explainable trail (0:00)

- Visual: Original QualCanvas brand frame
- On-screen callout: Your committee should be able to follow how the interpretation developed
- Narration: For a doctoral researcher, the challenge is not only coding the material. It is retaining enough context and methodological reasoning to explain the analysis months later to a supervisor, examiner or future reader.

### 2. Start from the method—not the software (0:15)

- Visual: captures/08-methodology.png
- On-screen callout: Use the methodology field guide to clarify the analytical stance
- Narration: QualCanvas includes a public methodology field guide covering several approaches and ethics in practice. Use it as orientation, then follow the literature, supervision and protocol that govern your study.

### 3. Keep chapters of evidence connected (0:29)

- Visual: captures/13-canvas-workspace.png
- On-screen callout: Sources, codes and memos remain visible in one analytical space
- Narration: The canvas helps you keep excerpts, categories and memos connected while the study grows. Use separate canvases or clear groups for phases when one workspace becomes harder to explain than the research itself.

### 4. Memo for your future self (0:44)

- Visual: captures/16-memo-menu.png
- On-screen callout: Record why a choice made sense at the time
- Narration: Write memos that capture the reason for changes in the codebook, sampling decisions and developing claims. These notes become valuable when drafting the methods and findings chapters.

### 5. Bring a reviewable codebook to supervision (0:56)

- Visual: captures/23-codebook.png
- On-screen callout: Definitions and examples make feedback concrete
- Narration: A supervisor can give better feedback when code names are supported by definitions and excerpts. Review the codebook together without treating code frequency as a substitute for analytical importance.

### 6. Export for writing and examination (1:10)

- Visual: captures/20-export-menu.png
- On-screen callout: Reports, workbooks and visuals support different readers
- Narration: Use reports for drafting, Excel for structured review and canvas images for explaining relationships. Remove or protect participant material before sharing any export outside the approved research environment.

### 7. Write from the trail you actually made (1:25)

- Visual: Original QualCanvas brand frame
- On-screen callout: Method · evidence · decision · revision · claim
- Narration: The most defensible dissertation describes the process that actually happened. Keep the method, evidence, decisions, revisions and claims connected from the beginning, and the final writing becomes more honest and more manageable.
